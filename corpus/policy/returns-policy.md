# **Returns Policy**

## **Purpose**

This policy defines the standard return rules for products sold through the company’s direct commerce channels. It is intended for support, commerce operations, and fulfillment-adjacent teams who need to answer return-policy questions consistently.

## **Standard Return Eligibility**

Customers may request a return within 30 calendar days of delivery for most products purchased through the company’s direct commerce channels.

To qualify for a standard return:  
\- the order must have been delivered within the last 30 calendar days  
\- the item must have been purchased through a supported direct commerce channel  
\- the item must not be listed as final sale or non-returnable  
\- the return request must include a valid order reference or other support-verifiable purchase information

## **Non-Returnable Items**

The following items are not eligible for standard return unless required by law or unless a documented exception applies:  
\- final sale items  
\- personalized or customized items  
\- gift cards and stored value products  
\- items with clear signs of misuse beyond normal trial or inspection  
\- hygiene-sensitive items that were opened, worn, or otherwise compromised when the product category requires sealed or unused condition

## **Damaged Product Exception**

Products reported as damaged on arrival or damaged during normal initial unpacking are not handled as standard discretionary returns. They are handled under the damaged-product exception process.

A damaged-product case can qualify for exception handling when:  
\- the customer reports visible damage, functional failure, or material defect at first use or first inspection  
\- the reported issue is consistent with transit damage, manufacturing defect, or packaging failure  
\- the item condition and timeline do not suggest extended wear, misuse, or unrelated damage after normal use

When a damaged-product case qualifies for exception handling, support should follow the Damaged Product Handling SOP rather than applying the standard return path without review.

## **Approved Return Outcomes**

**Depending on** product condition, order status, and policy fit, the approved outcome may be one of the following:  
\- refund to original payment method  
\- replacement order  
\- store credit where permitted by policy or operational guidance  
\- no return authorization when the request is not eligible

Replacement should be favored when:  
\- the product is damaged or defective within policy  
\- inventory is available  
\- the customer prefers replacement and no fraud or abuse concerns are present

Refund should be favored when:  
\- replacement inventory is unavailable  
\- the customer declines replacement  
\- the product category or issue type makes replacement operationally inappropriate

## **Required Verification**

**Before** finalizing a damaged-item return outcome, support should collect or verify the following when practical:  
\- order identifier  
\- product name or SKU if available  
\- issue description in customer language  
\- whether the package arrived visibly damaged  
\- whether the item was used beyond initial inspection  
\- photos when required by the Damaged Product Han**dling SOP**

## **Escalation Thresholds**

Support should escalate a return-related case when any of the following applies:  
\- product damage appears to affect multiple customers or indicates a broader incident  
\- the product belongs to a restricted, high-value, or high-risk category defined in operational guidance  
\- the customer is requesting an exception outside the documented damaged-product process  
\- system behavior, label generation, refund processing, or replacement workflows are failing  
\- there is disagreement between policy fit and recommended operational action

Escalations related to workflow or operational failure should follow the Incident Escalation Procedure when appropriate.

## **Customer-Facing Guidance**

Support responses should be clear, direct, and consistent with policy. Agents should avoid promising specific financial outcomes until eligibility and issue type have been verified.

Approved language should make these points clear:  
\- standard returns and damaged-product exceptions are different workflows  
\- eligibility depends on product type, order timing, and issue details  
\- additional documentation may be required for damaged-item cases  
\- some cases may require operational review before final approval

## **Policy Ownership and Review**

This policy is owned by Commerce Operations. Support leadership and fulfillment operations should review the policy at regular intervals or after major process changes affecting returns, replacements, or exception handling.
