# **Incident Escalation Procedur**e

## **Purpos**e

This procedure defines when and how an internal user should request escalation for an operational incident. It applies to support, commerce operations, engineering support, and designated approvers.

## **When Escalation Is Required**

**An** escalation request should be created when one or more of the following is true:  
\- customer-facing impact is sustained or growing  
\- a mitigation attempt failed or produced only partial recovery  
\- multiple teams or leadership coordination is required  
\- policy, workflow, or operational exceptions require managerial approval  
\- the incident may affect revenue, trust, or significant customer experience metrics

## **Who May Request Escalation**

The following roles may request escalation:  
\- support\_analyst  
\- engineering\_support  
\- ops\_manager  
\- admin

## **Who May Approve Escalation**

The following roles may approve or reject escalation**:**  
\- ops\_manager  
\- admin

## **Required Escalation Inputs**

Every escalation request should include:  
\- incident code or incident identifier  
\- concise business and customer impact statement  
\- current incident status  
\- reason escalation is needed now  
\- proposed priority or urgency level  
\- brief summary of mitigation already attempted *if applicable*

## **Escalation Priority Guidance**

Use the following general priority guidance:  
\- high: active customer-facing issue with meaningful business impact or failed mitigation  
\- medium: material operational issue requiring coordination but not currently severe customer outage  
\- low: issue requires review or exception approval but is not time-sensitive

## **Approval Decision Standards**

Approvers should consider:  
\- current customer impact  
\- breadth of affected channel, region, or workflow  
\- risk of delay versus risk of unnecessary escalation  
\- whether the issue is already controlled or genuinely worsening  
\- whether the requester provided enough supporting detail

## **Approved Outcome**

If the escalation is approved:  
\- record the approval decision  
\- notify the relevant owner or leadership path according to team norms  
\- attach the escalation reference to the incident or case context  
\- continue incident handling under the escalated path

## **Rejected Outcome**

If the escalation is rejected:  
\- record the decision notes clearly  
\- keep the current incident or case under standard handling  
\- tell the requester what additional evidence would justify reconsideration if applicable

## **Documentation Requirements**

Every decision should be traceable. The system should record:  
\- who requested escalation  
\- who approved or rejected it  
\- when the decision occurred  
\- the reason provided by the requester  
\- the final decision notes

## **Ownership and Review**

This procedure is owned by Commerce Operations and should be reviewed after major incident retrospectives or changes to approval responsibilities.
