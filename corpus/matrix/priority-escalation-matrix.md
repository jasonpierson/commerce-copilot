# **Priority Escalation Matrix**

## **Purpose**

This matrix provides a simple decision aid for choosing escalation priority. It is intended to support consistent incident-escalation requests and approver decisions.

## **Priority Levels**

The supported priority levels are:  
\- high  
\- medium  
\- low

## **High Priority Criteria**

Use high priority when one or more of the following is true:  
\- the issue is actively customer-facing and affecting revenue-generating workflows  
\- mitigation failed or only partially reduced impact  
\- multiple channels, regions, or teams are affected  
\- support volume is rising quickly during an active incident  
\- delay in escalation is likely to increase customer harm or business risk

## **Medium Priority Criteria**

Use medium priority when:  
\- the issue has material operational impact but limited current customer harm  
\- coordination is required across functions, but the issue does not meet criteria for high priority  
\- data reliability or workflow quality is materially degraded and review is needed soon  
\- escalation is justified, but the issue is not rapidly worsening

## **Low Priority Criteria**

Use low priority when:  
\- the issue requires managerial review or exception approval  
\- current customer impact is limited or contained  
\- the request is precautionary rather than urgent  
\- standard handling can continue while review occurs

## **Approver Expectations**

Approvers should review:  
\- impact severity  
\- breadth of affected scope  
\- mitigation status  
\- evidence quality  
\- urgency of coordination needs

## **Usage Notes**

This matrix is guidance, not a replacement for judgment. If evidence is incomplete but early signals suggest broad customer impact, the priority should favor timely review rather than waiting for perfect certainty.

## **Ownership and Review**

This matrix is owned by Commerce Operations and should be reviewed after major incident retrospectives or changes to escalation responsibilities.
