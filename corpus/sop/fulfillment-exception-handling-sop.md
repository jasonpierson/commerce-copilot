# **Fulfillment Exception Handling SOP**

## **Purpose**

This SOP defines how support and operations teams should handle fulfillment-related exceptions that fall outside normal order, shipping, or replacement workflows. It is intended to support consistent decision-making and escalation.

## **When to Use This SOP**

Use this SOP when a case involves:  
\- repeated damaged deliveries that suggest a handling or packaging issue  
\- shipment exceptions that may require operational review rather than standard support handling  
\- conflicting fulfillment signals, such as delivered status with clear non-receipt evidence  
\- a pattern that may affect multiple customers, products, or locations

## **Exception Types**

Common fulfillment exceptions include:  
\- repeat damage from a specific fulfillment path  
\- suspected packaging failure  
\- shipment delivered but product missing or incomplete  
\- replacement order blocked by a fulfillment constraint  
\- fulfillment-center-specific handling anomaly

## **Verification Steps**

1\. Confirm the order and shipment context.  
2\. Review customer description and any supporting images.  
3\. Check whether the issue is isolated or part of a larger pattern.  
4\. Review current inventory and replacement feasibility.  
5\. Check for active incidents or operational notices affecting fulfillment.  
6\. Decide whether the case can be handled normally or requires escalation.

## **Decision Rules**

A fulfillment exception may remain in standard handling when:  
\- the issue is isolated  
\- the outcome is clear under existing policy  
\- no broader operational signal is present

A fulfillment exception should be escalated when:  
\- multiple customers report similar damage or shipment anomalies  
\- the issue points to a specific location or packaging process  
\- operational review is required before refund, replacement, or exception approval  
\- the case intersects with an active incident or unresolved platform problem

## **Documentation Requirements**

Case notes should capture:  
\- order and shipment reference  
\- product involved  
\- exception type  
\- evidence reviewed  
\- outcome selected  
\- escalation reference if applicable

## **Ownership and Review**

This SOP is owned by Commerce Operations and should be reviewed after fulfillment incidents, carrier exception trends, or packaging-process changes.
