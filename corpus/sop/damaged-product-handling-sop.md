# **Damaged Product Handling SOP**

## **Purpose**

This SOP defines the internal handling process for cases where a customer reports that a product arrived damaged or appears defective during first inspection or first use. It is intended for support analysts, commerce operations, and other internal teams responsible for damaged-item re**view.**

## **When to Use This SOP**

Use this SOP when a customer reports any of the following:  
\- visible physical damage on arrival  
\- packaging damage that appears to have affected the product  
\- missing or broken components discovered during first inspection  
\- product failure or defect at first use that is consistent with manufacturing or transit issues

Do not use this SOP for:  
\- general fit or preference returns  
\- late dissatisfaction unrelated to defect or transit damage  
\- ordinary wear after extended use  
\- clearly unsupported warranty or misuse claims that do not fit damaged-item review

## **Required Inputs**

Before choosing an outcome, collect or verify the following whenever practical:  
\- order identifier  
\- customer-reported product name  
\- product SKU if available  
\- delivery date or order completion date  
\- issue summary in customer language  
\- whether the packaging arrived visibly damaged  
\- whether the product was used beyond initial inspection  
\- supporting photos when required by category or issue severity

## **Photo Requirement Rules**

Photos should be requested when:  
\- the issue is not clear from the written description  
\- the product is high value or operationally sensitive  
\- the requested outcome is replacement without return  
\- visible shipping damage may indicate a broader fulfillment or carrier issue  
\- the case may need escalation to operations or engineering support

Photos may be optional when:  
\- the issue is already evident from prior verified evidence  
\- the item category has low fraud risk and the issue type is straightforward  
\- system guidance explicitly permits no-photo handling for low-risk damage reports

## **Verification Steps**

1\. Confirm the order exists and was purchased through a supported direct commerce channel.  
2\. Confirm the product is not excluded by policy.  
3\. Determine whether the report describes likely transit damage, packaging failure, missing parts, or product defect.  
4\. Review photos or other supporting details if required.  
5\. Determine whether replacement inventory is available.  
6\. Check for known incidents, known product issues, or recent fulfillment exceptions that may explain the damage pattern.  
7\. Select the appropriate outcome or escalate if policy fit is unclear.

## **Outcome Rules**

Support may choose one of the following outcomes based on verified case facts and policy fit:  
\- replacement  
\- refund  
\- store credit where specifically allowed  
\- deny return or deny replacement when the case does not qualify  
\- escalate for operational review

Choose replacement when:  
\- the product qualifies under damaged-item handling  
\- inventory is available  
\- the item does not require special review  
\- no broader exception blocks replacement

Choose refund when:  
\- the product qualifies but replacement inventory is unavailable  
\- the customer declines replacement  
\- the case meets policy but replacement is operationally inappropriate

Choose escalation when:  
\- there are signs of repeat damage affecting multiple orders  
\- the product category is restricted or high value  
\- support cannot determine whether the issue fits policy without ops review  
\- system workflows such as label generation, replacement ordering, or refund execution are failing  
\- the pattern suggests a broader fulfillment, packaging, or platform issue

## **Operational Checks**

Before finalizing an outcome, support should check the following internal conditions when possible:  
\- current inventory availability  
\- known incident or outage affecting return processing or replacement ordering  
\- known quality issue for the product or batch  
\- known fulfillment-center issue affecting damaged shipments

If an active incident may affect the case, reference the incident in the case notes and follow the applicable runbook or escalation process.

## **Documentation Requirements**

Case notes should include:  
\- concise issue summary  
\- product and order reference  
\- whether photos were requested or reviewed  
\- final outcome selected  
\- rationale for exception handling if the outcome was not standard  
\- any related incident or escalation reference

## **Escalation Rules**

Escalate to operations or the designated approver when:  
\- the same product is repeatedly reported damaged across multiple cases  
\- the issue could indicate a fulfillment center handling problem  
\- refund or replacement requires exception approval  
\- the case may affect broader customer communications or incident handling

If escalation is required due to an active operational issue, follow the Incident Escalation Procedure.

## **Customer Communication Guidance**

Support should communicate the outcome clearly and avoid implying guaranteed resolution before verification is complete. If evidence or review is still pending, the customer should be told what is being reviewed and what information is still needed.

## **Ownership and Review**

This SOP is owned by Commerce Operations and should be reviewed after major policy changes, known damage-pattern events, or return-processing incidents.
